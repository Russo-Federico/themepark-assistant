# Future World — Accessibility Guide

## Wheelchair & Mobility Aid Access

Future World is fully navigable by standard and motorized wheelchairs. All pathways are paved and level, with the exception of a slight incline on the approach to Neon Circuit (ramp assistance available on request from any Lab Technician).

### Attraction-Specific Access

**Orbit Station**
- Wheelchair transfer required to board the ride vehicle
- Transfer seat available at boarding gate
- Companion seating adjacent to transfer point
- Waiting time for accessible boarding: typically 5–10 minutes

**Neon Circuit**
- Wheelchair transfer required
- Mobility aids stored securely at boarding platform
- Guests must be able to support their own upper body
- Not recommended for guests with back or neck conditions

**Data Dive**
- Fully wheelchair accessible — no transfer required
- Dedicated accessible vehicle available on every cycle
- Companion seating included in accessible vehicle
- Recommended as first attraction for guests assessing mobility needs in the park

## Sensory Considerations

- Data Dive has a low-stimulation mode available for sensory-sensitive guests — request at the entrance
- Neon Circuit involves strobe lighting effects; guests with photosensitive conditions should consult medical advice before boarding
- Quiet zone located on the east side of Data Dive, marked with a green quiet zone sign

## Companion Pass

Guests who require a non-paying carer to accompany them on attractions are entitled to a Companion Pass, available free of charge at Guest Services (Main Gate or center hub). The pass allows one companion to board alongside the guest at no additional ticket cost.

## First Aid

Nearest first aid station: Future World east corridor, adjacent to Orbit Station plaza.
