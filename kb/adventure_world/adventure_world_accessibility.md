# Adventure World — Accessibility Guide

## Wheelchair & Mobility Aid Access

Adventure World pathways are fully accessible, though some sections near Thunder Rapids involve uneven stone-effect surfacing. Smooth alternative routes are clearly marked with blue accessibility signs throughout the area.

### Attraction-Specific Access

**Thunder Rapids**
- Wheelchair transfer required to board the circular raft vehicle
- Transfer point is located at the boarding dock — Expedition Guides assist with all transfers
- Mobility aids stored in a secure, dry locker during the ride (guests receive a key)
- Note: guests will get wet. Waterproof covers for mobility aids available on request.

**Peak Assault**
- Wheelchair transfer required
- Guests must be able to independently support their upper body and grip the overhead restraint
- Not recommended for guests with shoulder, back, or neck conditions
- Due to the suspended coaster format, foot clearance at boarding requires Expedition Guide assistance

**Jungle Trek**
- Fully wheelchair accessible — no transfer required
- Dedicated accessible boat available, with wider boarding ramp
- Companion seating included in accessible boat
- Slowest and most relaxed ride in the area — recommended starting point for guests assessing comfort

## Water Ride Considerations

Thunder Rapids involves significant water exposure. Guests with electronic medical devices (cochlear implants, insulin pumps, etc.) should consult with First Aid before boarding. Waterproof pouches available at the boarding dock.

## Companion Pass

Available free of charge at Guest Services. Entitles one companion to board alongside the guest at no additional ticket cost on all Adventure World attractions.

## First Aid

Nearest first aid station: Adventure World south entrance, adjacent to Base Camp Grill.
